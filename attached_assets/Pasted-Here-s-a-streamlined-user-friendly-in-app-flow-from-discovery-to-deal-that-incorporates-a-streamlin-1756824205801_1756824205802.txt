Here's a streamlined, user-friendly in-app flow—from discovery to deal—that incorporates a streamlined legal draft tool for NZ property transactions. It’s broken into three phases: Quick Start (Phase 1), Legal Review (Phase 2), and Streamlined Signing (Phase 3).

Phase 1: Fast Draft Flow — "Draft an Offer"

1. Discover & Decide

User browses properties via the Tinder/TikTok-style swipe deck.

When they see a home they’re serious about, they tap "Make an Offer".

2. Fill Offer Details

A modal/form appears requesting:

Buyer names

Contact info

Offer price

Conditions (e.g., financing, building inspection)

Settlement period

Additional comments

3. Generate Draft Agreement

Upon submission, the app auto-fills a draft Purchase & Sale Agreement (or Lease if applicable) with entered details.

User reviews, then clicks Download as PDF/Docx or Send to lawyer.

A brief disclaimer displays:

"This is a draft. Please review with a lawyer before signing."

Phase 2: Legal Review & Finalization

4. Lawyer Partner Integration

The app introduces two options:

"Review with Cribsy-approved Lawyer"

Flows to a form: user pays a flat-fee (e.g., $199) and submits the draft securely.

"Use My Own Lawyer"

User downloads the draft, sends it directly to their lawyer outside the app.

5. Lawyer Feedback Loop

If using Cribsy’s partner lawyer:

Lawyer reviews draft, updates clauses, and uploads final version in the user’s account.

User receives a notification.

6. Download & Sign

User downloads the lawyer-approved agreement.

They handle signing externally (e.g., in person or via electronic signing like DocuSign).

Phase 3: Digital Signing & Delivery (Advanced)

7. Digital Signing Integration (Future)

Digital Signing Launch: Integrate an NZ-compatible e-signature provider (e.g., RealMe or DocuSign).

User signs in-app.

Copies are stored securely for all parties.

Email confirmations and digital timestamps ensure legal compliance.

8. Closing & Notifications

Once signed, all parties receive a copy.

Cribsy triggers a follow-up, like linking to conveyancing services or reminders for settlement dates.

Value Proposition at Each Stage

Phase 1: Users get something tangible in minutes—a draft agreement they wouldn’t otherwise have.

Phase 2: Adds legal assurance—“draft done right”—and convenience via partner lawyers.

Phase 3 (optional, future): Truly seamless end-to-end real estate closing from swipe to signed contract.
